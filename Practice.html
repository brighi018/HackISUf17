Practice

Build a path to help with mental illness. Select the activities you like the best and create a path to success. Note: We know that talking to a professional can be hard so we tried to supply ‘steps’ that someone can do by themselves. It is not the best option but is a great first step someone can take. These steps are meant to manage, not ‘cure’, someone’s illness.

Build Your Own Path

Select from the steps below to start your own path.

Go on a Vacation

Taking a trip can help you get out of stressful situations and allow you to relax. (Depression, Anxiety)

Volunteer at an Animal Shelter

Working with animals can help relieve stress and also will help the animals stay happy. (Depression, Anxiety)

Try Out A New Yoga Class

Practicing deep breathing and mindfullness can help your focus and to clear your mind. (Depression, Anxiety)

Pop Bubble Wrap

Bubble wrap is fun to pop and also can clear your mind. Try using it whenever you get upset or need a distraction.

Build A Pillow Fort

Take some time to yourself and build a pillow fort. Watch Netflix or a classic Disney movie. (Depression, Anxiety)

Declutter A Room

Cleaning out a room can improve your mind and help bring a new feeling. (Depression, Anxiety)

Paint With Bob Ross

Watch and paint with Bob Ross. Add the new painting to your house. (Depression, Anxiety)

Make A DIY Project

Take some time to yourself and make a diy project. (Depression, Anxiety)

Talk to a friend

Find the person you trust the most and tell them what you are experiencing. They will be able to support and encourage you on your journey to recovery. (Anoerxia)

Practice A Hobby

Whenever you are placed in a stressful situation, practice your hobby to balance out the bad vibes. (Anorexia)

Post Positive Reminders

Write positive notes somewhere you will see it often; it is important to remember and think positive things to get rid of the bad ones. (Anorexia)

Throw out things that hold you back

Getting rid of what is only making your situation worse is the most important step to getting better. This could be fitness/fashion magazines or even spending less time around people that constantly diet. (Anorexia)
